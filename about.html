Kinzie, Lead Singer

Kinzie, the dynamic front man of Stage Fright, is the energetic driver of their electrifying performances and soul-stirring music. With powerful vocals, magnetic stage presence, and deep connections with fans, Kinzie has become an icon for the raw energy and sincerity of modern rock. Influenced by family and friend, her lyrics most often concern resilience, rebellion, and passion. Off-stage, Kinzie is an ardent advocate for emotional support animals, further solidifying their rock icon status with a heart.

Carter, Lead Guitarist

Carter is the lead guitarist for Stage Fright and mastermind behind the signature sound of the band. Renowned for their electrifying solos, innovative riffs, and unparalleled technical skill, Carter brings both fire and finesse to every performance. Inspired by legends like Jimmy Page, their playing seamlessly blends raw emotion with precision, captivating audiences worldwide. Offstage, Carter is a passionate composer and gear enthusiast, constantly pushing the boundaries of their craft. With his guitar at hand, Carter is the quintessential embodiment of rock and roll, leaving an indelible mark on every stage on which he performs.

Rory, Lead Drummer

Rory is the powerhouse drummer of Stage Fright, the heartbeat that underscores their dynamic sound. Known for blowing minds with raw energy, perfect precision, and innovative rhythms, Rory gives each song intensity beyond their wildest expectations. Taking a cue from the master that is Kinzie, this band has combined technical mastery and a raw, uninhibited passion to drumming, epitomizing the music that comes forth from Stage Fright. Offstage, Rory is a passionate mentor and music lover who shares their knowledge to inspire the next wave of drummers. With each beat, Rory proves they are the pulse of rock and roll.

Orgins of Stage Fright:

Stage Fright was born out of a shared passion for music and teenage rebellion, when three high school friends named Kinzie, Carter, and Rory decided to channel their energy into creating something unforgettable. Raised in New Castle, the three would spend most nights huddled in Rory's garage, where restlessness evolved into raw, electrifying melodies. What started out as a jam session to break the tedium of small-town living grew into a band. The band members named themselves Stage Fright, which was about the nervousness they faced earlier when performing live-a thing they overcame together at their first small gig at a local coffeehouse. That vulnerability and genuineness found its place in audiences, and word started spreading about them. Classic rock, punk, and modern alternative influences blend into the sound of Stage Fright, which is both nostalgically familiar and refreshingly new. It reflects youthful determination brought them together in the first place. From high school talent shows to headlining local festivals, their journey stands as a testament to the power of friendship and pursuing dreams.
